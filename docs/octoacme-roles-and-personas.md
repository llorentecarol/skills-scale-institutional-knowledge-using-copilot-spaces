# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers are responsible for shaping the visual and interactive experience of products. They ensure that interfaces are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs while collaborating closely with product and engineering teams.

### Responsibilities
- Design project interfaces, wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Ensure user-centric design principles are applied throughout the product lifecycle
- Participate in usability reviews and user research sessions
- Collaborate with Product Managers and Project Managers on design requirements and feasibility
- Facilitate design handoff to Developers with clear specifications and assets

### Goals
- Deliver consistent, accessible, and engaging user experiences
- Reduce friction and increase product usability
- Bridge the gap between user needs and technical implementation

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions with Product and Engineering
- Handoff documentation and annotated mockups shared with Developers
- Participation in sprint planning to estimate design tasks and flag dependencies

---

## Technical Writer / Documentation Specialist

### Role Summary
Technical Writers maintain, update, and standardize process and project documentation. They ensure that knowledge is captured accurately, communicated clearly, and accessible to all team members and stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain, update, and standardize process and project documentation
- Draft release notes, user guides, and internal knowledge base articles
- Coordinate with stakeholders to ensure accuracy, completeness, and clarity of documentation
- Support onboarding by keeping role guides and process docs current
- Participate in retrospectives to identify documentation gaps

### Goals
- Ensure documentation is accurate, consistent, and easy to find
- Reduce knowledge silos and onboarding friction
- Enable repeatable, well-documented project execution

### Typical Communication
- Collaboration with all roles to gather technical and process details
- Regular reviews with Project and Product Managers for content accuracy
- Contributions to retrospectives and onboarding sessions

---

## QA Lead / Quality Engineer

### Role Summary
QA Leads define and oversee the quality strategy for projects, ensuring that features meet acceptance criteria before release. They coordinate testing efforts across manual and automated channels to deliver reliable, high-quality software.

### Responsibilities
- Define the test strategy, test plans, and quality gates for each project phase
- Oversee test case creation and execution across functional and regression areas
- Coordinate manual and automated QA efforts across the team
- Work with Developers during feature handoff to ensure testability
- Collaborate with Product Managers to validate acceptance criteria

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Increase test coverage and automation efficiency
- Maintain clear quality standards and reporting

### Typical Communication
- Feature handoff meetings with Developers and Product Managers
- Test reports and bug triage sessions
- Sprint reviews and release readiness assessments

---

## DevOps / Infrastructure Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps and Infrastructure Engineers maintain the build, release, and deployment pipelines that enable teams to deliver software reliably. They provision and manage infrastructure while enforcing best practices for reliability, security, and scalability.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain and improve CI/CD build and release pipelines
- Provision and manage cloud and on-premise infrastructure
- Enforce best practices for system reliability, security, and performance
- Support Developers with environment configuration and troubleshooting
- Coordinate release scheduling and deployment logistics with Project Managers

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and repeatable software delivery
- Minimize downtime and infrastructure-related incidents
- Maintain secure, compliant, and cost-efficient infrastructure

### Typical Communication
- Environment and deployment updates shared with Developers and Project Managers
- Incident postmortems and infrastructure reviews
- Release coordination meetings and on-call rotation planning

---

## Stakeholder Advocate / Customer Success

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Advocates represent the voice of business stakeholders and end users throughout the project lifecycle. They act as the primary point of contact for key customers, ensuring that their needs are heard and addressed in product decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Represent business and user needs in planning and prioritization sessions
- Act as primary point-of-contact for major customers or end user groups
- Collect, synthesize, and relay user feedback to Product and Project teams
- Ensure that delivery outcomes align with customer expectations and business goals
- Track adoption and satisfaction metrics and escalate concerns as needed

### Goals
- Close the feedback loop between customers and the delivery team
- Increase customer satisfaction and product adoption
- Ensure business value is realized from delivered features

### Typical Communication
- Regular syncs with Product Managers and Project Managers on customer priorities
- Customer feedback summaries and satisfaction reports
- Participation in planning sessions and release reviews to align on outcomes
